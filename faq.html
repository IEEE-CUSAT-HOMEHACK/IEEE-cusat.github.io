  • General

  • What is HomeHack?

    Homehack is a 5-days online hackathon organized by the IEEE SB CUSAT and IEEE MEC SB.

  • What is IEEE?

    The IEEE Student Branch provides opportunities to meet and learn from fellow IEEE Student and Graduate Student Members and engage with professional IEEE members locally. An active IEEE Student Branch can be one of the most positive elements of your academic career, offering programs, activities, and professional networking opportunities that build critical skills outside of the classroom. IEEE currently has Student Branches at thousands of universities and colleges in hundreds of countries throughout the world.

  • Who can attend Homehack?

    All university undergraduate students are welcome to be a part of the hackathon.
    All you need to have are your College ID Cards for verification.

  • IS there any participation fees?

    No, this event is totally free of cost.

  • I am a beginner in technology, Can I participate in Homehack?

    We will have mentors online to teach you lots of new skills and guide you towards creating your first hack. All the students are very friendly and willing to help each other out. Do join us and learn your first lines of code—it could be the beginning of learning something cool!

  • What are the procedures to submit a project?

    we’ll be using Github for the submission of projects.

  • Can I participate without a computer science background?

    Of course! It all depends on your creative skills and willingness to learn. You can learn about new technology anytime, anywhere.

  • what is the selection procedure?

    our selection team will pick you on the basis of the details provided by you at the time of registration.

  • How do I contact you?

    Email us at:ieeesb@cusat.ac.in

  • What are the perks of participating in Homehack?

    The hackathon aims at increasing students' knowledge and skill level. Most importantly, you get an amazing platform to interact and socialize with the tech community. Cash prizes and other perks are also in store for the winners. The best part of course, is the swag that comes with being in a hackathon.

  • First-year exclusive track

  • What is the first-year exclusive track?

    One of Homehack’s vision is to motivate first-year students to come forward and be a part of hackathons. To appreciate first years' skills, we have divided our track into two subpaths.
    a. Arduino/Raspberry based projects
    b. UI/UX designing or prototyping

  • Rules & Regulations

    1. Do mention in the registration form if the track reserved for first years has been selected. Your team members should only consist of first years if so.
    2. If your project is cool enough to secure the top three positions in a general category, the First Year Appreciation prize will be awarded to another deserving team.
    3. A College ID for verification is strictly required.

  • can a team win both first or second prizes and the Evan prize?

    No. Our rules suggest that if any team from the first-year exclusive track are able to secure the top two positions in the general category, they will not be considered for Evan prize. We wish to be as fair as possible and inclusive of all talents.

  • do we need to be first-year students of engineering colleges only?

    Not at all! First-year students of any under-grad course are free to register themselves for this category.

  • Daily Challenges

  • Where will the daily challenges happen?

    We’ll be using some of the very common platforms like CodeChef, hackerrank and others for the technical challenges.

  • Will the challenges include coding only?

    no, there’ll be some non-technical challenges too—quizzes, debates and many more.
